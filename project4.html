Our Idea

Hologram Box from Recycled Cardboard

To build an innovative hologram projection device using waste material, promoting the concept of upcycling and demonstrating the potential of bio-cost educational tools for visual learning and entertainment.

Materials Required

  • Waste cardboard for the box structure.
  • Transparent sheet for the hologram projection.
  • Black sheet for light control.

Costing

  • Utilizing only waste and available materials.

Process

  • Constructed a sturdy box frame using recycled cardboard pieces.
  • Receiver Coil: Place another coil within the magnetic field to receive energy.Cut a transparent sheet into a pyramidal shape and attached it securely inside the box to create the holographic effect.
  • Lined the inside of the box with a black sheet to reduce light interference and enhance the visual quality of the hologram.
  • Decorated the outer box with colors to make it visually appealing.

Implications

  • This hologram box serves as an engaging tool to explain scientific concepts and enhance interactive learning. It can be used in classrooms to demonstrate the properties of light and visual effects, as an educational and entertainment tool

Future Scope

  • Exploring ways to improve the projection clarity and image resolution.
  • Investigating how similar holographic devices can be developed for educational purposes in underfunded schools.
  • Experimenting with different sizes and shapes of transparent sheets for diverse holographic effects.
