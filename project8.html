Study Lamp: Waste to Innovate

To create a functional study lamp using waste materials promoting sustainable waste management.

Materials Required

  • Waste plastic bottle
  • Discarded pipe
  • Unused plastic cup
  • Electrical wire
  • Switch
  • Recycled Bulb
  • Chargeable battery

Costing

  • Battery: ₹15
  • Battery Holder: ₹5
  • Switch & Wire: ₹20
  • Total: ₹40

Process

  • Collected a discarded plastic bottle and cut it to form the lamp's base and body.
  • Repurposed an old plastic cup to shape the lamp's shade
  • Attached a waste pipe to connect the shade to the base, ensuring stability.
  • Wired the recycled bulb, connected it to a battery source, and installed a switch for functionality.
  • Painted the lamp for a refined appearance, making it an attractive addition to any study space.

Implications

  • The project highlights the potential of waste management through innovative reuse, reducing plastic waste, and raising awareness about sustainable practices among students and the community.

Future Scope

  • Incorporating solar-powered energy to make the lamp more eco-friendly.
  • Enhancing the design for increased durability and aesthetic appeal.
  • Using smart lighting technology to make it more energy-efficient.
